Abstract

   This document provides information about widely used in Ukrainian 
   Internet community, including Ukraine and worldwide Ukrainian 
   speaking community, character encoding koi8-u for mail and news 
   exchange as well as for presentation WWW information resources in 
   Ukrainian language. KOI8-U (KOI8 Ukrainian) is de-facto standard 
   widely accepted by Ukrainian Internet community with resources on it 
   published at many sites, including encoding tables, fonts, and 
   locale support for many operating systems and environments. KOI8-U 
   is compatible with KOI8-R (RFC 1489) in all Cyrillic letters and 
   extends it with four Ukrainian letters which locations are compliant 
   with ISO-IR-111. KOI8-U charset tables in RFC1345 ISO 9945-2 formats 
   are given in Appendixes. 
   

Introduction

   This documents provides information about widely used in Ukrainian 
   Internet community character encoding for mail and news exchange as 
   well as for presentation WWW information resources in Ukrainian 
   language.

   Though the proposed character encoding "KOI8-U" is not currently an 
   international standard, there is a large Internet user's community 
   (including Ukraine and worldwide Ukrainian speaking community) 
   supporting it.

   Originally, specification of proposed standard koi8-u was officially 
   adopted by the conference of Postmasters of Ukrainian Internet Service
   Providers in Slavsk in Autumn of 1992 presented by Igor Sviridov from 
   Kiev and Stas Vorony from Kharkiv. Later in June 1995 this specification 
   was completed with UKRAINIAN GHE WITH UPTURN.

   "KOI8-U" (KOI8 Ukrainian) is de-facto standard accepted 
   by all Ukrainian community in the Internet and unofficially 
   published at many sites 
   (F.E., ftp://ftp.ua.net/pub/info/encodings/koi8-u/ukr_chars_in_koi8-
   u_and_others.txt; http://www.gu.net/multiling/koi8-u/). 

   KOI8-U intends to solve the problem of recently reintroduced 
   Ukr. letter GHE (with upturn) now missed in old codepages like 
   ISO-IR-111, ISO 8859-5. This character is included into Unicode 
   Character Set (UCS) as Cyrillic GHE with upturn (0490 - capital, 
   0491 - small). and is a part of Ukrainian language alphabet.


MIME character set name: koi8-u


Relation to other RFCs

   This standard is unpublished but based on several published 
   standards: first of all, RFC1489 (it is fully compatible in all 
   Russian letters), RFC-1345, ISO 8859-5, ISO-IR-111, ISO 10646.


Compatibility with other character sets

   The lower part (decimal #0-#127) of the Ukrainian Character Set 
   KOI8-U is a perfect copy of ASCII, just as it's used in KOI8-R and 
   other non-ASCII codepages.

   The upper (decimal #128-#255) part of the KOI8-U Character Set contains
   all Russian letters defined in KOI8-R and four Ukrainian letters 
   (#164, #180 - ukr. ie, #166, #182 - ukr. i, #167, #183 - ukr. yi, #173,
   #189 - ukr. ghe  with upturn) which locations are compliant with ISO-IR-111.

   BOX DRAWINGS  in the rest of positions (those not used by Ukrainian 
   letters) are the same as in KOI8-R character set. List of all 
   differences between KOI8-R and proposed KOI8-U is given in APPENDIX A


Specification of the upper part of KOI8-U codepage

   The description of all characters from the upper half part of KOI8-U 
   codepage is taken from ISO 10646 Unicode Character Set. All Russian 
   letters are left on their original KOI8-R places. Introduced Ukrainian

APPENDIX A

DIFFERENCE OF KOI8-U from KOI8-R (RFC 1489)

   KOI8-U is compatible with KOI8-R in all Cyrillic Letters and 
   completes it with four Ukrainian letters UKRAINIAN IE #164, #180, 
   CYRILLIC SMALL LETTER BELORUSSIAN-UKRAINIAN I #166, #182, 
   UKRAINIAN YI #167, #183, UKRAINIAN GHE (WITH UPTURN) #173, #189.
